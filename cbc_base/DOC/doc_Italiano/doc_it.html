ClipByClip is a tool to practice the comprehension of a language.

It can be used in active mode by listening, reading, repeating
or in passive mode by endlessly listening to a few lines of subtitles while busy doing something else.
It can also be useful in your native language to memorize for instance a poem, a piece of prose or a list of terms.

It's suggested to

  1. Select a few lines of subtitles to play by clicking on the §arrowRight§ icon of the first line and the §arrowLeft§ of the last line.
  2. Hide these lines (click to change the §openedbook§ icon to §closedBook§) and play them one (click on the §talkingHead§ icon) or several times (§infinity§ icon).
  3. If it's useful you can reduce the playback speed (vertical slider).
  4. Try to repeat what you hear
  5. Show the lines (click to change the the §openedbook§ icon to §closedBook§) and play them again.
  6. To see the translation click on the §hideTran§ icon to change it to §showTran§.
  7. You can play and practice a line at time
Of course you can just enjoy the video as usual from te beginning to the end with or without subtitles using the buttons below the video.
To watch the film without interruptions click on the button with the little arrow inside a circle which is just below the video.

To read the subtitles in the original language, click on the book close icon, and on the open book to hide them.

To read the translated subtitles, click on the t? icon and to hide them click on the T icon.
